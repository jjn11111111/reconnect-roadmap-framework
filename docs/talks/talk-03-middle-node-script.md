# Talk 3 — The Middle Node
## Full word-for-word script (~18 minutes)

**Title on stage:** *The Middle Node — Where Two Truths Make a Whole*  
**Series:** re:CONNECT — The Power of −0−  
**Tone:** Colloquial, warm, precise. Not preaching. Inviting.  
**Stage note:** Pause where marked. Silence is part of the talk.

---

### [0:00 — COLD OPEN]

*(Stand center. No slide yet. Beat of two seconds.)*

You know that argument you've been carrying around?

Maybe it's with a person. Maybe it's with yourself. Maybe it's with the whole damn world.

And every time it comes up, there's this pressure — this invisible referee — telling you to pick a side.

Are you the good one or the bad one? The smart one or the naive one? The awake one or the asleep one?

*(Small pause.)*

What if the referee is wrong?

What if the real game isn't about winning a side at all?

What if the real game is about finding the middle — and staying there long enough to remember who you are?

*(Slide 1: title — The Middle Node)*

I'm Jeremy. I build maps. Not maps of streets — maps of *patterns*. Patterns that show up in your body, in light, in sound, in the way you argue with your brother at Thanksgiving.

And tonight I want to give you one pattern that changed my life.

It's called the middle node.

And once you see it, you can't unsee it.

---

### [2:00 — ONE LAW, MANY FACES]

*(Slide 2: spectrum bar — red · green · blue with − · 0 · +)*

Here's the whole thing in one picture.

Every spectrum you can name has two poles… and a center.

Not a compromise center. Not "meet in the middle" like politicians pretending to care.

A *hinge*. A razor. A still point that both sides measure from.

Science and spirit.

Fire and water.

War and peace.

Sorrow and joy.

Fake and authentic.

*(Gesture left, center, right.)*

Left pole. Center. Right pole.

Three nodes. One continuum.

In chemistry, pH seven is the center between acid and base.

In light, green sits in the middle of what your eye can see.

In an electrical circuit, zero volts is the reference between plus and minus.

*(Beat.)*

Nobody owns the center.

That's the first clue.

---

### [4:00 — THE TRIPARTITE SPELL]

*(Slide 3: three dots — + · 0 · −)*

When I was younger I thought power meant *picking the right side*.

Be on the side of truth. Be on the side of love. Be on the side of the people who finally get it.

And if you pick right hard enough, long enough, loud enough — you win.

*(Pause. Softer.)*

That strategy breaks people.

Because every pole, pushed to its extreme, becomes a distortion.

The savior who burns out trying to rescue everyone.

The empath who drowns in everyone else's weather.

The narcissist who makes every room orbit their hunger.

The cynic who calls destruction "realism."

Different costumes. Same mistake.

They all left the center.

*(Slide 4: two thrones, empty middle)*

Patriarchy isn't the answer.

Matriarchy isn't the answer either.

Those are just two thrones on opposite sides of the same war.

The answer isn't a throne.

It's the middle node.

---

### [6:30 — THE CAKE PARADOX]

*(Slide 5: simple text — "Have cake? Eat cake?")*

You've heard the old riddle.

You can't have your cake and eat it too.

Fine. Cute. Depression-era wisdom.

But stay with me.

You can also have *no cake* and *not eat cake*.

You can have *all the cake* and eat *all the cake*.

And — here's the part that matters — you can stand in the bakery, look at the whole display, and realize you were never required to enter the argument about shelves.

*(Half smile.)*

Most of us live our entire lives in the bakery, screaming about which shelf is moral.

We never notice the doorway.

The middle node is the doorway.

Not escapism. Not apathy.

*Presence without possession.*

---

### [8:30 — TWO TRUTHS AT ONCE]

*(Slide 6: mirror image — palindrome word "level")*

There's a word in English that reads the same forward and backward.

Level.

L-E-V-E-L.

I didn't invent this. Language did.

And somewhere in the back of my head, for years, I kept feeling: *that's not a party trick. That's a law.*

Two halves. One whole.

Wholeness isn't picking one half and amputating the other.

Wholeness is holding the paradox without flinching.

You can be broken and healing at the same time.

You can be furious and grateful in the same breath.

You can love someone and need distance from them in the same season.

Both true.

Not comfortable.

*True.*

The middle node is where both truths are allowed to exist without a courtroom deciding which one gets to live.

---

### [10:30 — LIVE EXERCISE]

*(Slide 7: black slide. Two words only: "Find the hinge.")*

Alright. I'm going to ask you to do something with me.

If you're willing.

Don't perform for your neighbor. Don't worry about looking spiritual or looking skeptical.

Just try it.

Bring to mind one conflict. One polarity you've been living inside.

Maybe it's political. Maybe it's personal. Maybe it's that voice in your head that says you're not enough while another voice says you're too much.

Got it?

*(Pause three seconds.)*

Now — without fixing it, without solving it, without picking a winner — ask yourself:

*Where is the center of this?*

Not the compromise.

Not the fantasy where everyone apologizes in slow motion.

The center.

The hinge.

The place where you can see both poles clearly and neither one owns you.

*(Lower voice.)*

Breathe into that center.

*(SILENCE — 10 full seconds. Do not rush. Count them.)*

*(After silence, barely above a whisper:)*

That's the middle node.

You just visited it.

You can come back.

---

### [12:30 — WHAT CHANGES WHEN YOU LIVE THERE]

*(Slide 8: torus outline, soft — empty center highlighted)*

When you practice this — not once on a TED stage, but daily, messy, imperfect — something shifts.

You stop needing to be the hero of every room.

You stop needing to be the victim of every room.

You stop outsourcing your worth to whichever side applauds you loudest.

You start telling the truth sooner.

You apologize without self-erasure.

You set boundaries without cruelty.

You become, in a word my project uses a lot…

*(Beat.)*

…authentic.

Not loud-authentic.

*Centered-authentic.*

Become authentic isn't a brand slogan for me.

It's a direction.

Inward first. Then outward.

---

### [14:00 — SELF FIRST, THEN ALL]

*(Slide 9: three lines, large type)*

There's a sequence I learned the hard way.

Compassion for yourself first.

Forgiveness for yourself first.

Love for yourself — complete, unglamorous, Tuesday-morning love — *first.*

Then, and only then, the widening.

Compassion for the person who hurt you.

Compassion for the person you hurt.

Compassion for the roles people play in the script — including the ones you don't like.

Not because everyone's behavior is fine.

It's not.

Because hatred is a pole.

And poles pull you off center.

---

### [15:30 — THE HIGH ROAD ISN'T UP]

*(Slide 10: vertical arrow crossed out; horizontal line through center)*

People say "take the high road."

I used to think that meant ascend.

Climb above the mess.

Float in moral altitude.

I don't think that's the high road anymore.

The high road is *in*.

Into the middle.

Into the zero point where both truths coexist and you don't have to pretend only one is real.

The high road isn't up.

It's *in*.

---

### [16:30 — BRIDGE TO THE SERIES (OPTIONAL IF STANDALONE, TRIM IF NOT)]

*(Slide 11: five words — Zero · Mirror · Middle · Numbers · Sound)*

If this map interests you, there are other lenses.

Zero as portal.

The mirror in biology and vibration.

The numbers that breathe in six-step cycles you can check on your phone.

Sound as geometry you can hear.

I'm not asking you to believe a cosmology tonight.

I'm asking you to remember one thing you already did ten seconds ago.

You found the hinge.

---

### [17:30 — CLOSE]

*(Slide 12: single line)*

**The power isn't in the pole. The power is in the middle.**

*(Beat. Eye contact. No rush.)*

You don't have to win the war between your poles.

You have to return to center often enough that the war loses its fuel.

That's the middle node.

That's −0−.

Thank you.

*(Hold two seconds. Walk off without over-explaining.)*
